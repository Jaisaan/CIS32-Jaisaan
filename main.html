Christ The Redeemer

This historic statue, named "Christ the Redeemer", is located in Rio De Janiero, Brazil. This statue was built in the image of Jesus Christ, created by a French sculptor named Paul Landowski. Constructed in 9 years between 1922 and 1931, the statue is 98 feet high (not including the 26 foot pedestal that is rests on). Weighing in at 635 metric tons, Christ The Redeemer is located on top of the Corcovado mountain in the Tijuca National Park, overlooking Rio De Janeiro. Christ The Redeemer has served as a stout symbol of Christianity all over the world, and has become a cultural icon in Rio De Janeiro.

Who: Created by French sculptor Paul Landowski and built by Brazilian engineer Heitor da Silva Costa, in collaboration with French engineer Albert Caquot. Romanian sculptor Gheorghe Leonida fashioned the face.
What: A strong global symbol of Christianity as well as one of the Seven Wonders of the World.
When: Constructed in 9 years between 1922 and 1931
Where: Rio De Janeiro, Brazil. On top of the Corcovado mountain in the Tijuca National Park, overlooking Rio De Janeiro.
Why: The Catholic Circle of Rio proposed the idea of the statue based on the quality of life at that time, which they perceived as "Godlessness" in the society.
How: Christ The Redeemer was built using reinforced concrete and soapstone. The statue is 98 feet high (not including the 26 foot pedestal that is rests on), and weighs in at 635 metric tons
